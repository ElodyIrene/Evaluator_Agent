# Open Source Evaluation Metrics Knowledge Base

## OpenRank

OpenRank measures the influence of an open-source project in the developer ecosystem.

### Why it matters

A higher OpenRank usually means the project has stronger ecosystem influence, more attention from developers, and more interactions with other open-source projects.

### Suitable scenarios

OpenRank is useful when evaluating mature infrastructure projects, AI frameworks, developer tools, and widely adopted libraries.

### Limitations

OpenRank should not be used alone. A project may have high influence but still have maintenance risks, slow issue response, or weak documentation.

---

## Activity

Activity measures how active a project is over time.

### Why it matters

A highly active project usually has frequent updates, ongoing development, and active maintenance.

### Suitable scenarios

Activity is useful for judging whether a project is still alive and whether maintainers are actively improving it.

### Limitations

High activity does not always mean high quality. It may also come from bug fixes, churn, or unstable development.

---

## Contributors

Contributors measures how many people contribute to the project.

### Why it matters

A healthy contributor base can reduce maintenance risk and improve long-term sustainability.

### Suitable scenarios

This metric is useful for evaluating community health and whether the project depends on a small group of maintainers.

### Limitations

Contributor count does not show contribution quality. A few core maintainers may still contribute most of the important work.

---

## Bus Factor

Bus factor estimates how many key contributors would need to leave before the project becomes difficult to maintain.

### Why it matters

A low bus factor means the project depends heavily on a small number of people, which creates sustainability risk.

### Suitable scenarios

Bus factor is useful for evaluating infrastructure projects, AI frameworks, and projects that may be adopted in production.

### Limitations

Bus factor should be interpreted with contributor count and recent activity. A high bus factor does not guarantee strong governance.

---

## Issue Response Time

Issue response time measures how quickly maintainers respond to newly opened issues.

### Why it matters

Faster response time usually means better user support and more active project maintenance.

### Suitable scenarios

This metric is important for projects with many users, frameworks, SDKs, and developer tools.

### Limitations

A fast first response does not mean the issue is solved. It should be combined with issue resolution duration.

---

## Issue Resolution Duration

Issue resolution duration measures how long it takes to close issues.

### Why it matters

Shorter resolution duration usually indicates better maintenance efficiency and issue handling capacity.

### Suitable scenarios

This metric is useful for evaluating project maintainability and support quality.

### Limitations

Some issues are complex and naturally take longer to solve. This metric should not be judged without context.

---

## Change Request Response Time

Change request response time measures how quickly maintainers respond to pull requests.

### Why it matters

Fast pull request response improves contributor experience and encourages community participation.

### Suitable scenarios

This metric is useful for judging whether a project welcomes external contributions.

### Limitations

Fast response does not always mean high-quality review. It should be combined with merge rate and contributor growth.

---

## Stars

Stars show how much attention a project receives on GitHub.

### Why it matters

Stars are a simple popularity signal and often reflect developer awareness.

### Suitable scenarios

Stars are useful for quickly estimating adoption and public interest.

### Limitations

Stars do not prove production readiness, maintenance quality, or technical quality.

---

## Forks

Forks show how many developers copied the project for reuse, modification, or contribution.

### Why it matters

Forks may indicate developer interest and potential contribution activity.

### Suitable scenarios

Forks are useful for evaluating reuse potential and community engagement.

### Limitations

Many forks may be inactive. Fork count should not be treated as direct evidence of active contribution.

---

## License

License defines how the project can be used, modified, and distributed.

### Why it matters

A clear open-source license reduces legal uncertainty and helps users adopt the project safely.

### Suitable scenarios

License is important for production adoption, enterprise usage, and commercial integration.

### Limitations

A permissive license does not guarantee project quality, security, or maintenance.
